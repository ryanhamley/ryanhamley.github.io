About

I'm a software engineer at Mapbox in San Francisco, where I help maintain the open-source web mapping library GL-JS. Previously, I worked on autonomous UAV mapping at Skycatch. I specialize in Javascript, WebGL, front end development, mapping and data visualization.

I live in Berkeley, CA with my wonderful wife, Amy and our shih tzu Perro. When not writing code, I'm usually reading, playing ice hockey and listening to music.

Conservation Project Tracker

Conservation Project Tracker is a collaborative resource for sharing information on land and water restoration and protection projects. This project allows organizations and governments to find partners in need of funding and vice versa.

  • Client: Lower Susquehanna Center For Land and Water
  • Date: September 2014
  • Service: Web Development
  • Built With: Angular, Mapbox, Leaflet, Bootstrap, Sass

Foundation For Pennsylvania Watersheds Grants

Foundation For Pennsylvania Watershed Grants is a project that allows FPW to track their grant spending. By opening the website to the public, FPW Grants fosters transparency in the environmental non-profit sector.

  • Client: Foundation For Pennsylvania Watersheds
  • Date: February 2014
  • Service: Web Development
  • Built With: Angular, Mapbox, Leaflet, Bootstrap, Sass

FracTracker

FracTracker is a mobile application that allows users to submit oil and gas photos or reports regarding drilling near them. Users can also view a map of nearby wells drilled and save their submissions for future reference. This map shows both wells that have been drilled unconventionally and conventionally.

Available for iOS and Android on the App Store and Play Store.

  • Client: FracTracker
  • Date: iOS: May 2014 / Android: October 2014
  • Service: Mobile Development
  • Built With: iOS: Objective-C, MapBox with MapKit, Magical Record, AFNetworking /
    Android: Java, MapBox, Retrofit, Butterknife, Picasso, SugarORM

Baltimore Harbor Water Alert

Baltimore Harbor Water Alert allows the public to monitor the contamination levels of 22 locations on the Tidal Patapsco River and its tributaries. A map and interactive charts allow users to see the latest levels of 12 different readings. You can read more about the website at The Baltimore Sun's B'More Green blog.

  • Client: Blue Water Baltimore
  • Date: July 2014
  • Service: Web Development
  • Built With: Angular, D3, Mapbox, Leaflet, Bootstrap, Sass

The Homeowner's Guide to Stormwater

The Homeowner's Guide to Stormwater helps users create their own stormwater management plan and select simple stormwater solutions to be implemented on their property. Interactive maps allow users to draw the outlines of their property and various details of the property to be used in calculating stormwater runoff and savings.

  • Client: Penn State Agriculture and Environment Center
  • Date: March 2014
  • Service: Web Development
  • Built With: Angular, Mapbox, Leaflet, Bootstrap, Sass

Water Reporter

Water Reporter allows people to use their smartphone or tablet to document areas of concern or interest in their local waterways. Using real-time geolocation, a user can view reports in their area and submit new reports to a local volunteer. You can read more about the project and see submitted reports at the Water Reporter website.

Available for iOS and Android on the App Store and Play Store.

  • Client: Chesapeake Commons
  • Date: iOS: May 2014 / Android: October 2014
  • Service: Mobile Development
  • Built With: iOS: Objective-C, MapBox with MapKit, Magical Record, AFNetworking /
    Android: Java, MapBox, Retrofit, Butterknife, Picasso, SugarORM
